$0 to $5M, then an acquisition

Boston Institute of Finance: education for wealth-management firms, registered investment advisors and banks. Regulated finance, where a compliance mistake costs the license, not the deal. The company had essentially no business when we took it over.

By the end I ran it as director of revenue operations and de facto COO. Everyone except the CEO ran on my plan, and everyone was ten people, counting the admin and the accountant. Product, demand generation, retention, reporting, budget: one owner.

Revenue went from nothing to $5M, Colibri Group acquired the company, and I led the integration. Ten people to $5M in a regulated market. The plan was mine, and so was the machine under it.

Six brands, one brain

Today I am Director of Business Operations for a $100M unit at Colibri Group, professional education in regulated industries. My direct team is about 25. Roughly 150 people across marketing, sales and engineering run on the plans my team sets.

The six brands all live in financial services, and they are still drastically different businesses: wealth management, insurance, banking, each with its own model, workflow and vocabulary. No amount of status meetings keeps that in one head. So I built the unit a brain. An AI model is a good reasoner and a terrible place to store the truth, so the brain is not the model: it is a plain, versioned knowledge base the agents reason against, and whatever a conversation teaches me is written back the same day. It compounds. Documents start accurate. And because shared numbers are the same discipline, a large part of my job is making six brands agree on one set of them.

A market map that refreshes itself

One build in full. I needed to rank the insurance-education market by licensed agents per company. My first thesis was wrong: I ranked by agent dropout, the data would not carry it, and I killed it. The rebuilt version is a table in Clay, a go-to-market data tool, that reads the Texas regulator's public feed, aggregates per company, and re-pulls monthly. Nobody maintains it. It surfaced 46 carriers selling through one distributor, and that set the segmentation and the outreach.

How I lead

Clear is kind. Disagreement means we go find the data. No data means we run a test, and the test settles it. If I am wrong, I am wrong, and the team knows it. That is why the wins carry weight.
